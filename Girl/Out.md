# Tips on Coming Out

How to come out as trans? Here's my list of tips and suggestions:

* Personal safety comes first.
* Tell one person at a time.
* Begin with those you trust most, and who are important to you.
* Stay in control of your own coming-out by asking people not to tell others before you've told them you're ready (later).
* For those who you're not sure of, but are important (e.g. parents with influence over your college fund) maybe test the waters by polling their politics first or mentioning you met someone who's trans and asking their advice.
* Concerning work, know your rights and how to enforce them on the off chance that something goes pear-shaped. Every part of the English-speaking world has laws in your favor, at least as far as work is concerned.
* Have a safety plan and a buddy with you if you plan on coming out to people who are potentially adverse to the idea.
* Be prepared for those closest to you to take it hard, demand explanations, mourn the loss of (the idea of) a son/daughter/pet. It's not your job to explain or justify, but pointing them at some PFLAG resources might be nice.
* Realize you don't have to come out to everyone.
* Realize that eventually you *will be* out to everyone in your existing community. Going stealth may require a change of job, neighborhood, etc.
* Realize that *most people don't care.*
