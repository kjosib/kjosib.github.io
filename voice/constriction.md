# Constriction / Noise / Hiss

Key concepts:
* Breathing and Glottal Control
* Tension in the Vocal Tract
* Other Notions

## Breathing and Glottal Control

Initial explorations around deep breathing.
Experiment with extended and very light F and SH sounds to get prepared.

Alternate light FFF and light VVVV at G3 looking for no gravel / waver / instability - FFFFFF-inhale-VVVVV - This is a SOVTE application.

Use measured, monotone speech to simplify the practice as you add speech in.

Be mindful of e.g. glottal events.

Slide pitch *up from below* because it may be easier to find a clearer tone at a lower pitch.

## Tension in the Vocal Tract

Allow tongue to flop forward, hanging limp like a panting dog might do.
Breathe gently and slowly allow tension to melt over the course of *minutes*.
Be mindful of all parts of your vocal tract, from the shoulders to the nose.
Notice where twitches and tensions come in over time. Acknowledge, then release.


## Other Notions

Use vibrato to find something relaxed. Vibrato is the canary in the coal mine.

Nice long drones shifting the vowel (or size, or both?) can be like *wax-on-wax-off.*
