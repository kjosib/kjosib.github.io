# The Mental Side of Voice Training

You get out of this what you put into it.

Voice training is exactly as fun or as agonizing as you want it to be.

If you think of it as drudgery to be tolerated, then that is what you will experience.

If your vocal tract is your new fun play toy that makes all kinds of weird and wonderful noises you can explore and learn to produce at will,
then you will have a happy fun time playing games with your voice.

I know which version of life I'd rather live.

------

**Story time:**

Long, long ago in a city far, far away, I learned a foreign language in service to my government.
For 18 months, it was literally my job to learn the Korean language.
My teachers would periodically remind me of something akin to what's written above.
I will confess I never developed a true passion for Korean during the course of my employ with the government,
but I certainly appreciate the Korean culture, history, art, and indeed the media coming out of the Republic of Korea.
Today, the Korean language is having a growing impact worldwide, so there's more opportunity to hear it and see it in a social context.

Speaking a foreign language is like a sport: You can't simply memorize vocabulary words;
you have to get out there and use the language! Badly at first! A lot!

I did not have many conversations in Korean,
but I can say that I picked up more skill at *understanding the language in real-time* from
media by and for Korean people *that I enjoyed watching.*

I haven't used Korean professionally (or, really, at all) in many *many* years,
but to this day the bits I learned from TV shows remains unforgettable and easily accessible.
The bits I tried to jam into my skull for the test are completely gone.

**Why is this relevant?**

Trans voice is also like a sport:
To get any good at it, you have to *do the thing,* and badly at first.
Therefore, it's best to find ways of doing the thing that make it enjoyable!
