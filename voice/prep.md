# Environmental and Technical Preparation

**You'll get the most out of voice training in a proper environment.** The following are essential:

* Physical and Psychological safety: Are you in a place and time where it's safe for you to present with an altered voice?
* Quiet and private room: We need to be able to hear nuances of your vocal performance, and you need to be able to hear ours.
* Permission (including from yourself) to make weird mouth-noises and talk in funny voices: They're part of the process.
* Be well-hydrated and have a good supply of drinking water close at hand.

**Quality hardware also helps:**
I recommend a respectable-quality headset with built-in microphone, ideally with side-tone in hardware.
The average gamer's headset is plenty, but I would be skeptical of anything that doesn't fully cover your ears.
I'd actually suggest *not* to bother with a fancy separate microphone like what podcasters are often seen with.
They tend to pick up more background noise due to their greater distance from your mouth.

Also, there are some **useful software programs** to have installed and functional on your computer:

* [Audacity](https://www.audacityteam.org/download/): This is great for making, playing, and manipulating recordings of your own voice, which is a big part of training.
* The desktop client for Discord is technically optional but highly recommended.



# Baseline Recording

In advance of in-person work, please make a recording of [Comma Gets a Cure](https://www.dialectsarchive.com/comma-gets-a-cure).
Use the voice you're currently most confident with.
This will serve as a point of reference for comparison so you can see how far you've come at any time.

You are welcome to record using Audacity, but if you're unfamiliar with it, then any tool is fine.
Windows and Mac both come preinstalled with a built-in voice recording application.

I'll ask that you share the recording - ideally in advance.
