# Why no gender-monitoring apps

There are apps out there that claim to measure pitch (and perhaps other variables) to estimate vocal gender.

**That's not how any of this works!**

You can't make a "40% fem" voice just by pitching James Earl Jones up to the A3-C4 zone - a range I have zero doubt he could have reached just fine (before he joined the Choir Invisible) because in addition to being the voice of Darth Vader and CNN, he was also an accomplished baritone singer and *not a single member of his audience* was ever going to even think his voice was the least bit unmanly even for a syllable.

Yes, pitch is part of the program and a necessary step in completing a trans voice. But vocal gender is not a weighted average of components. It's a symphony. Each component must play its correct part in proper coordination with the others.

On a deeper level, my beef with apps like these is that they seem to aim to bypass the notion of ear-training. There are at least two important reasons for ear-training. First is to deal with the fact that you can't be running a voice-monitoring app at all times and places - that's weird and clocky. It's better to be able to correct yourself in real-time based on what your ears are already telling you. The second reason is that a trained ear tends to suffer less vocal dysmorphia. If you know what you're listening for, you can hear the components in your voice and *know objectively* that you're doing all the right things (or which bits to adjust) rather than suffering from a vague feeling of "can't/won't-ever-pass". With trained ears, you can know how close you are to your goal and what it takes to get there. You can work your strengths and your weaknesses at will and using zero equipment.

If I involve an application (or any exeternal tool) in a training program, the point is to help a learner develop their own
intuitive sense of managing a particular component.
