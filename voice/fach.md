# What the Fach?



Not a strange question at all. Buzzing in different parts of the body - typically the chest, neck, head, or face - can be a personal indicator that you're doing a new thing with some vocal parameter, but the buzzing is not *in itself* a goal or a useful interpersonal metric. Different people experience vocal change differently. There are terms in the wild - typically singing communities classically trained according to European operatic tradition - that use phrases like "chest-voice" and "head-voice". A significant minority of humans do experience *some* consistency in these effects, so that a vocabulary could develop around it, but trans-voice needs to work for *everyone,* not just those who fit neatly into the Fach system.
